Interesting Facts About Camels

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat

Interesting Facts About Bears

  • Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals. They have far superior navigation skills to humans; excellent memories; large brain to body ratio; and use tools in various contexts from play to hunting.
  • Bears grieve deeply for others. Cubs are known to moan and cry when separated from their mothers. This can go on for weeks if their mothers are killed by hunters
  • Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing. They can smell food, cubs, a mate or predators from miles away

Interesting Facts About Pandas

  • The average lifespan of a wild giant panda is 15-20 years. Captive giant pandas can live up to 30 years. The record age for a captive giant panda is 38 years
  • Pandas have sometimes been seen rolling down slopes. While they may be playing, they may also be trying to dislodge twigs from their fur
  • Pandas do not run fast—a slow trot is as fast as they can go. The fastest bear is the black bear, which can run 35 miles per hour. That’s about as a fast as a horse or deer

Interesting Facts About Flying Cicada

  • A cicada’s body color can be black, brown and green.
  • Annual cicadas emerge every year, while periodic cicadas emerge every 13 to 17 years.
  • Cicadas are separated into two classifications, annual cicadas and periodic cicadas.
